Vegetables

Our Vegetables Journey!

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, nicknamed "Little England" due to its cool climate and colonial architecture is a prominent vegetable farming center in Sri Lanka. The regions fertile soil and moderate climate are suitable for growing carrots, leeks, potatoes and other cool-weather vegetables.

Dambulla

Dambulla, in the Central Province, is known for its enormous vegetable cultivation. The areas's rich plains and irrigation systems promote the cultivation of a wide range of crops, including tomatoes, beans and brinjals.

Jaffna

Jaffna,located in the Nothern Province, is known for its distinctive farming practices and vegetable types. The region's sandy soil and arid climate are suitable for growing crops such as red onions, chiles and a variety of leafy greens.

Kandy

Kandy, located in the central highlands, is well-known for both its historical and cultural significance, as well as its booming vegetable farms. The region's mountainous terrain and temperature climate are ideal for growing a wide variety of crops, including cabbages, beans and bell peppers.

Badulla

One of Sri Lanka's most important vegetable-producing regions is Badulla, which is in the Uva Province. Vegetabes such as pumpkins, cucumbers and radishes can be grown in the district due to its diverse climate and altitude.
